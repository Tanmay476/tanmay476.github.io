About Me

Tanmay Shukla is a Computer Engineering student at Georgia Tech with a strong interest in FPGAs, hardware systems, embedded design, and high-performance computing. His technical experience spans FPGA development (VHDL/RTL), PCB design, and programming in C, C++, Python, Java, SQL, and RISC-V. At Georgia Tech Experimental Rocketry, he leads the avionics hardware team for a two-stage rocket targeting the Kármán line—designing the ADCS flight computer, integrating sensors and microcontrollers, and developing flight control algorithms with real-time telemetry. With RoboJackets, he worked as a Radio/RF Engineer, creating communication hardware for robots in the Robocup competition, where the team placed 4th in Division B internationally. Tanmay’s past projects include independent research on graphene supercapacitors, an FPGA-based servo controller, an embedded arcade game, and a Bluetooth-controlled PID line-following robot that won a robotics competition. He thrives on building systems that merge hands-on hardware with intelligent software and is focused on pursuing future work in semiconductor design, chip architecture, and other advanced hardware domains.
