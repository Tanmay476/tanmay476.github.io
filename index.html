About Me

I'm a Computer Engineering student at Georgia Institute of Technology with a 4.0 GPA, passionate about pushing the boundaries of hardware design and high-performance computing.

As Avionics Hardware Lead at Georgia Tech Experimental Rocketry, I'm leading the design of custom PC104 embedded systems for the first collegiate two-stage rocket targeting the Kármán line (100km altitude). I designed and routed the ADCS flight computer PCB for flight control and telemetry, while co-developing over 1000 lines of embedded C and Linux code for control algorithms.

I'm contributing to HLSFactory at Georgia Tech's Sharc Lab, an open-source framework that uses machine learning to optimize chip design. I improved post-implementation QoR prediction accuracy by over 20% through design space expansion and cross-vendor synthesis. As a Design Verification Engineer with SiliconJackets, I develop SystemVerilog testbenches for RISC-V processors and complete full VLSI physical design workflows using Cadence Innovus.

Notable Achievements:

  • Built FPGA-based PWM controller in VHDL with 1.4° servo resolution and zero timing jitter
  • Won 1st place at national science fair (3000+ students) for graphene supercapacitor research demonstrating 40% higher energy density
  • Won 1st place robotics competition with PID line-following robot controlled via PS5 Bluetooth

I thrive on building systems that merge hands-on hardware with intelligent software. My expertise spans FPGA development, RTL design, PCB layout, embedded systems, and VLSI physical design. I'm pursuing opportunities in semiconductor design, chip architecture, and advanced hardware domains.
